- The meeting is being recorded.  Our code of conduct applies to this meeting.  https://github.com/Ecma-TC54/tg2/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md.

- Minutes from last meeting: approved.
- Summary
  - The participants reviewed and approved the agenda, which included updates on the TC54 and Package-URL websites, a discussion on the Software Component ID (SCID), and a proposal for a dedicated `git` package type.
  - Steve Springett presented plans to expand the TC54 website to host HTML versions of standards and more descriptive content.
  - Michael Herzog updated the group on the VERS specification restructuring and proposed changes to VERS component names to `type` and `constraint`.
  - Discussions on the SCID package type focused on simplifying the category to open-source (non-registry) and proprietary/commercial software, the need for a unified registry, and the importance of rapidly agreeing on a namespace.
  - Matt Rutkowski raised the issue of challenges in identifying AI models using the current PURL system.
- **Ecma Compliance and Meeting Logistics**: The meeting opened with a welcome and a reminder that the proceedings are subject to the Ecma meetings and TC54 code of conduct, with a link provided in the chat and running notes. The standard order of business was noted to include approving minutes and conducting a roll call.
- **Agenda Review and Participant Roll Call**: Michael Herzog added three topics to the agenda: a quick website update, a return to the software component ID (SCID) discussion, and a discussion on Git. Piotr P. Karwasz confirmed the Git discussion, while Matt Rutkowski noted they would be monitoring the meeting. Steve Springett mentioned two items: the TC54 website and a project proposed by Tom Alrich for increasing PURL adoption.
- **TC54 Website Updates and Expansion**: Steve Springett presented requests to expand the TC54 website in two ways: by adding the HTML version of the standards for browsing, similar to TC39, and by expanding the explanatory content for each standard. The proposed URL pattern for browsing standards would be `tc54.org/427` (for PURL).
- **TC54 Website Content Strategy**: The group discussed the risk of replicating information already present on individual standard websites, such as the Package-URL website, and the need to inform people about why they should care and get involved. The consensus was to provide a more descriptive summary of what each standard does on the TC54 site and then link out to the existing, more detailed websites, while also hosting the full standard for reading on `tc54.org`.
- **Drafting TC54 Website Content**: Steve Springett announced plans to develop the new content for the TC54 website over the next couple of weeks. This content will be forwarded to each Technical Group (TG) for review before publication, and the TGs will be notified when the HTML versions of the standards become available on the site.
- **Package-URL Website Updates**: Michael Herzog provided an update on the Package-URL website, noting that the latest iteration—currently in staging—includes a rewrite of the front-end to discuss PURL and VERS, as the previous version only mentioned PURL. The content related to specification tools has been moved to a new "getting started" section to prevent crowding on the homepage.
- **Website Navigation and Mobile Optimization**: The Package-URL website update included adding drop-down menus, which led to a discussion about whether this requires an extra click for navigation and the importance of mobile optimization. Steve Springett noted that the majority of traffic to the CycloneDX website, over 60%, is from mobile devices, reminding the group to test their responsive design.
- **VERS Specification Content Restructuring**: Michael Herzog updated the group on VERS, explaining that the long RST document is being split into multiple documents for easier reading, including an introduction and core specification. The goal is to make the specification content easier to find by separating it from background and "how-to" information.
- **Proposed VERS Component Name Changes**: Michael Herzog proposed changing the VERS ‘version scheme’ component to `type` and the ‘version constraint’ component to `constraint`, arguing that including "version" in component names is redundant. They plan to make a more formal proposal for these changes.
- **TC54 Ecma Markup Timeline and Assistance**: Steve Springett outlined the tight schedule for Ecma ratification, noting that CycloneDX 2.0 and the Transparency Exchange API (TEA) will be up for ratification in December, requiring significant tooling and Ecma markup work. They requested assistance with Ecma markup toward the end of the year, suggesting that the PURL standard could serve as a template for VERS.
- **SCID/Package Type Discussion Simplification**: The group began discussing the SCID package type, focusing on combining PURL types for private/internal packages and packages without a registry. Michael Herzog proposed simplifying the discussion by removing store-specific marketplace types (e.g., Apple Store, Google Play), since they can be handled by existing PURL types, and combining "internal use only" and "proprietary" types, as internal use is inherently proprietary.
- **Proprietary/Registry-less Package Type Strategy**: The group agreed to focus on two tracks: open-source software without a registry and proprietary/commercial software. Michael Herzog suggested coordinating industry feedback, perhaps through an external group like the SBOM Forum or an OWASP working group, to focus on commercial needs while keeping the final decision with TG2.
- **Registry Unification and License Statements**: Philippe Ombredanne stressed the need for a common and simple process for registering packages, arguing for a single, unified registry to avoid constant debate over whether a package is open source. Matt Rutkowski emphasized that the registry should always have a positive license attribute, suggesting that if a license is not found or recognized, it should default to "no license" or "all rights reserved".
- **License Detection and Neutral Terminology**: The group discussed using existing services, such as Clearly Defined, for providing license evidence or utilizing a placeholder license syntax in tools like ScanCode LicenseDB for commercial or proprietary licenses. Michael Herzog suggested moving towards a neutral term instead of "proprietary," as it can be perceived negatively.
- **Registry Resolution and Collaboration with TEA**: Piotr P. Karwasz explained that TEA is fully PURL compliant, suggesting collaboration between the TGs to use TEA (or a subset) to resolve metadata, such as finding the SBOM corresponding to a PURL. This approach could support two kinds of SCIDs: domains without a registry using an opaque string, and those with a TEA server.
- **SCID Naming and Expediting Namespace Definition**: The group generally agreed that "SCID" is not an ideal name for the package type. To meet interest from the Linux kernel, KDE, Gnome, and Apache Software Foundation communities, Philippe Ombredanne urged the group to quickly decide on a name and namespace.
- **PURL as a "Mail Address" Concept**: Michael Herzog explained PURL's philosophy using the analogy of a mail address, noting that the specificity of an address (or PURL) cannot be controlled and must adapt to the world as it is. The intent of PURL is to identify any general definition of software, which may or may not have a traditional package registry.
- **Next Steps for SCID/Package Type Naming**: Michael Herzog suggested creating an issue to decide on the new name and structure, potentially aiming for a decision by the end of March. Philippe Ombredanne preferred to first focus only on agreeing on the namespace, which is enough for the kernel community to immediately begin using PURL for vulnerability management and CVEs.
- **Apache Foundation Legal Review**: Matt Rutkowski raised concern about the involvement of the Apache Foundation, noting that any proposed work for Apache PURLs should be cleared with their legal team and approved by the board. Piotr P. Karwasz confirmed they would bring this up in their next board report.
- **Target Date for Namespace Usage**: Philippe Ombredanne proposed aiming for the end of March to begin using the namespace, with the initial goal of implementing it for the Apache Software Foundation (ASF) and potentially the Linux kernel, focusing exclusively on the namespace itself. Matt Rutkowski was invited to act as an industry representative to evaluate how the namespace registration approach functions at the top level, specifically avoiding the complexities of diving into lower levels of registration which can be challenging for large software companies. Matt Rutkowski confirmed they would be interested, noting their greatest concern is developing a meaningful way to represent identifiers for the growing number of AI models.
- **AI Model Identification Challenges**: Matt Rutkowski raised the issue that the current Package-URL (PURL) system is insufficient for identifying AI models, especially regarding those within platforms like Hugging Face, even though a package type already exists. The core concern is that within a single model repository, the same model name can contain multiple actual models, along with different configurations and tokenizers, and the unique combination of these three elements is not currently covered by PURL. Philippe Ombredanne mentioned an upcoming project starting in May or June to create a catalog of all known large language models (LLMs), including their license origin.
- **Addressing AI Model Identification**: The need for accurate AI model identification is critical for compliance with regulations such as the EU AI Act. Matt Rutkowski expressed that the debate with Jan Kowalleck concerning property namespace definition for hyperparameters and parameters is driven by these agency requests, as these details are recognized as part of a model's identity. Steve Springett confirmed that the taxonomy is in a good place after they restructured it and noted that the topic could be raised within the OWASP AI SBOM working group, which is currently working on generating CycloneDX from sources like Hugging Face.
- **Git Type Proposal**: Philippe Ombredanne noted that all meeting topics were covered, with the minor exception of discussing the `git` type, which they felt was not controversial as the community had already agreed it should be a PURL type. The consensus was to create a specific type for `git` rather than a generic Version Control System (VCS) or Software Configuration Management (SCM) type, and Jon Moroney had previously expressed willingness to submit a pull request for this type. Steve Springett confirmed they had not been following the specific issue but acknowledged the decision to have a dedicated type for major cases like `git`.
- **Time Zone Adjustments for Next Meeting**: The discussion briefly covered the upcoming daylight saving time changes and their impact on the meeting schedule. The participants agreed that the next meeting would maintain the same USA hour to make it convenient for the majority of the US attendees, despite the time shift occurring later in Europe. They noted that this decision would be convenient for those in the USA and received appreciation from Matt Rutkowski and Steve Springett, who share a time zone.
- The meeting was adjourned.
