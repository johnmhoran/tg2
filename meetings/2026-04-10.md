- **Meeting Commencement and Agenda Review**: The meeting for the TC54-TG2 task group focused on the standardization of Package-URL and VERS, began with a reminder of the code of conduct. The call notes from the March 27th meeting were approved without objection. Michael Herzog planned to discuss tests, while Philippe Ombredanne intended to discuss the Package-URL (PURL) registry and registration, although they anticipated not having time to cover the PURL registry.
- **Administrative Note on Recording Capabilities**: Michael Herzog noted that Adam Herzog indicated that Google Meet recording capabilities are now available on the AboutCode Google Workspace account, suggesting a possible switch in how meeting recordings are handled. This was an administrative point made before diving into the test discussion.
- **Test Coverage Statistics and Documentation Drafts**: Michael Herzog informed Philippe Ombredanne that John Horan had compiled statistics on the current test coverage, including elements related to specification and type levels, noting that most tests are expected to pass. Michael Herzog also announced the creation of a "drafts" folder under the repository's docs/ folder to publish rewrites of documents, such as the "how to parse" guide and the test document introduction, to make it easier to review full document changes outside of pull requests.
- **Clarifying Roundtrip versus Validation Tests**: The primary structural issue to be addressed was the difference between roundtrip and validation tests. Michael Herzog proposed that the conceptual difference is that a roundtrip test produces an output PURL string, while a validation test produces an output message but no PURL string. Philippe Ombredanne characterized the roundtrip test as "syntactic sugar" that combines parsing and building, and that it does not necessarily need to exist as a separate entity.
- **Consolidating Test Types and Naming Conventions**: Michael Herzog advocated for consolidating the test types, suggesting that validation and roundtrip tests could be merged into a single type, and proposing to rename "roundtrip" to "validation" because it is a more meaningful term. John Horan confirmed that this discussion was specifically about combining validation and roundtrip tests, not the core parsing and building tests which remain foundational. Michael Herzog ultimately proposed proceeding with a decision document to adopt "validation" as the name for the combined type.
- **Revisiting the Error Messaging Scheme**: The second main topic was the need to revisit the current error messaging scheme, which currently uses `expected_failure` (true or false) and `expected_failure_reason`. Michael Herzog argued for a more graduated messaging system, not unique to validation, offering three levels: Info, Warning, and Error, with Error matching an `expected failure` true condition.
- **Normalization and Base/Advanced Test Distinction**: John Horan raised a question regarding the extent of normalization permitted at the base level, specifically concerning case insensitivity and the stripping of leading/trailing slashes in the subpath, as the existing specification is ambiguous. Michael Herzog confirmed their understanding that base-level testing should be a strict pass/fail system, while advanced testing includes opportunities for fixing or transforming the PURL elements. Philippe Ombredanne clarified that normalization rules associated with a type must be applied even at the base level to ensure a canonical output, which is core to the PURL standard.
- **Decision on Error Messaging Levels**: The discussion returned to the messaging system, concluding that the minimum system should stick with `expected failure` (true/false) but enhance `expected failure reason` to include at least two tiers: Error and Warning. This more refined messaging is intended to make test cases more effective as communication to humans and offer optional detail for tools.
- **Granularity and Organization of Specification Tests**: Michael Herzog brought up a pending pull request suggesting that specification tests could be broken down and organized by PURL component, such as a subdirectory for `spec/namespace`. John Horan questioned the utility of this if a single PURL string, even with multiple errors, typically fails only on the first problem encountered during testing. Michael Herzog clarified that the grouping is for organizational purposes and that the tool developer decides whether to stop on the first error or collect all errors.
- **Changes to How to Parse Document**: John Horan mentioned they were removing references to checksums from the "how to parse" document because they are not supported in the current specification. Michael Herzog and Philippe Ombredanne agreed that the "how to parse" and "how to build" documents are very early, pre-specification elements of the documentation and require a fresh start.
- **Update on ISO Approval and SPDX**: Michael Herzog provided an update on reaching out for the schedule of ISO approval for ECMA-427, noting that the SPDX community is waiting for this status before upgrading their documentation appendix. Philippe Ombredanne noted that the SPDX community could reference the ECMA standard directly.
- **Discussion on PURL Registry and Naming Conventions**: The group briefly discussed the PURL registry and the potential conflict of the proposed name "sid" with the Debian testing version. Michael Herzog and Philippe Ombredanne agreed that the conflict is marginal and the name "sid" should be maintained. The mechanics of storing the registry metadata and the possibility of organizations validating PURLs based on their domain namespace were also mentioned.
- The meeting was adjourned.
