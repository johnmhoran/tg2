## Notes

- The meeting is being recorded.  Our code of conduct applies to this meeting.  https://github.com/Ecma-TC54/tg2/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md.
- Intro by Philippe, preliminary matters, proposed agenda.
- Minutes from last meeting: approved.
- See the PURL Spec Issues tab for links to current Strategic and Tactical issues.
- Summary
   - Participants reviewed conference presentations and addressed the increase in security advisories. They identified a need for better filtering of automated vulnerability reports.
   - Development of 1 new ABNF grammar for the standard is progressing. Teams are prioritizing infrastructure overhauls and parser rewrites for better validation.
   - The group discussed adopting Package-URLs for AI models and skills. Members decided to address the VERS standard during existing community calls.
- **Meeting Minutes Approval**: The meeting minutes from the last call were presented for review and considered approved as no objections were raised. Attendees were reminded that they are subject to the TC54 code of conduct and the package-url project code of conduct, which are the same.
- **Agenda Topics for Discussion**: Michael Herzog presented four topics for the agenda, which included discussing a separate monthly call for "VERS," an update on the ABNF grammar, potential SID updates, and test updates. Philippe Ombredanne noted that Michael's list covered their topics, and John Horan and Steve Springett did not have additional topics to bring up.
- **Eclipse Conference Announcement**: Philippe Ombredanne announced their attendance at the annual Eclipse conference, OCX, in Brussels, where they presented on Package-URL and its standardization. The presentation was well-received, leading to interesting contacts with organizations using Package-URL throughout their supply chain operations. A common topic of conversation at the conference was the implications of AI-generated code for open source.
- **Security Advisory Process and Vibe Coded PRs**: Steve Springett observed an uptick in security issues reported for CycloneDX, noting that establishing a funneling process for security advisories for the Package-URL organization would be advisable. Philippe Ombredanne agreed that distinguishing legitimate reports from garbage is difficult, exacerbated by numerous "vibe coded" pull requests across different repositories, which require time to review.
- **AI and Vulnerability Discovery**: The discussion turned to the use of AI, specifically mentioning the Mythos tool from Anthropic, for finding new security vulnerabilities at nearly zero cost. However, the cost of fixing and reviewing these findings, even with AI assistance for remediation, is expected to remain high. Steve Springett mentioned developing a Claude skill for finding and remediating security vulnerabilities, including secure design reviews and threat modeling, which they found to be highly effective.
- **Grammar Update and Community Meeting**: Michael Herzog provided an update on the ABNF grammar, noting a significant uptick in activity, and they asked John Horan to work on supporting it with testing. The grammar update will be on the agenda for the community meeting next week, as Jan Kowalleck will attend. The intent is to eventually add the grammar to the standard after it has been proofed and tested.
- **Testing and Parser Rewrite**: John Horan is working on a complete rewrite of the "how to parse" documentation, which is considered a necessary baseline update. The overall testing infrastructure requires a massive overhaul, as most existing test cases are success cases rather than failing cases, which is not ideal for testing the standard. John Horan noted the lack of a reliable validator in any language to run the existing test cases against the current specification.
- **Discussion on Standard Documentation Location**: Michael Herzog mentioned that Jan Kowalleck suggested putting "how to parse" and "how to build" documentation into the standard's annexes, but Michael is less convinced of this, feeling that the documentation is too dynamic for the standard. However, adding the grammar to the standard is considered a very tangible and appropriate change for a second edition. The grammar is viewed as a concrete, implementable fix for base-level character details and the use of ABNF is supported because Package-URL is a URL.
- **ANTLR Parser Development**: Daniel Scanteianu of Bloomberg is developing an ANTLR parser as a proof point related to the ABNF grammar. ANTLR is a tool used to create a parser based on a grammar, and Michael Herzog is encouraging this development as it makes sense. John Horan has also been working with Daniel and Jan Kowalleck on ensuring that unencoded colons are handled correctly in the proposed grammar for specific permitted areas.
- **AI Models and Package-URL**: Matt Rutkowski discussed the concept of "skills" for models, which are structured templates that inform how the context buffer is created, and these skills can be versioned and published. They pointed out that discussions are needed on creating Package-URLs for adapters and models, especially concerning compatibility and the evolving concept of skills. Philippe Ombredanne mentioned a project starting in June to index and ensure that models and their relationships on Hugging Face have proper Package-URLs.
- **Need for Dedicated VERS Focus**: Michael Herzog expressed concern about the lack of momentum on the "VERS" standard and proposed initiating a separate, monthly call to maintain focus on it. Steve Springett and Philippe Ombredanne agreed that while they are not in favor of more meetings, they want to see "VERS" ratified this year, and Philippe suggested trying to earmark 30 minutes in the next community meeting first.
- **Conda Package Security**: Philippe Ombredanne mentioned a request for a Conda Package-URL type and highlighted that there is no public open vulnerability feed for the 27,000 open-source Conda packages. Matt Rutkowski expressed interest in following this topic, acknowledging the significance of Conda in research.
- **Virtual Environment and Public Vulnerabilities**: Matt Rutkowski confirmed that all their examples are being pushed out using Conda as the assumed virtual environment. Steve Springett noted that the lack of a system of record for publicly disclosed vulnerabilities—neither in the NVD nor in GitHub security—is expected to become a much larger problem this year. This increase in complexity is attributed to the rise of AI and the complete collapse of the NVD.
- The meeting was adjourned.
